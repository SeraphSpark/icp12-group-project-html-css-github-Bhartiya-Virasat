Acharya Charaka - Father of Ayurveda

Quick Facts

Time: 1st-2nd century CE

Origin: Ancient India (likely in Kashmir region)

Field: Medicine, Ayurveda

Famous Work: Charaka Samhita

Background

Acharya Charaka was one of the principal contributors to Ayurveda, the traditional Indian system of medicine. He systematized and compiled ancient medical knowledge into the famous text Charaka Samhita, which is still studied today in Ayurvedic medicine.

Major Contributions

1. Charaka Samhita

  • One of the oldest and most authoritative texts on internal medicine (Kayachikitsa) .
  • Describes over 340 plant-based medicines and 120 surgical instruments.
  • Details over 1,500 diseases, diagnosis methods, and treatments.
  • Divides the human body into three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha ‚Äî the foundation of Ayurvedic health.

2. Preventive Healthcare

  • Emphasized the importance of balanced diet, hygiene, and lifestyle for disease prevention.
  • Introduced the concept of holistic healing‚Äîbody, mind, and spirit in harmony.

3. Ethical Practice

  • Outlined a code of ethics for Ayurvedic physicians ‚Äî compassion, responsibility, and purity of character.
  • Recognized the doctor as a servant of humanity rather than a trader of medicine.

Legacy

  • Known as the "Father of Indian Medicine."
  • His work formed the basis for Ayurveda, influencing Indian, Arabic, Persian, and Greek medicine.
  • Charaka Samhita remains a core curriculum in Ayurvedic medical education today.
  • Inspired generations of healers with his holistic approach to mind-body wellness .
