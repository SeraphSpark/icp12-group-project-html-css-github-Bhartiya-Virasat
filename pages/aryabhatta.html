Aryabhatta - Ancient Indian Genius

Quick Facts

Time: 476 - 550 CE

Birthplace: Kusumapura (modern-day Patna, Bihar, India)

Field: Mathematics & Astronomy

Famous Work: Aryabhatiya - written in 499 CE

Historical Context

Aryabhatta was one of the earliest Indian mathematicians and astronomers whose ideas influenced not only Indian science but also Islamic and European scholars centuries later. He lived during the Gupta Empire, considered the Golden Age of India. It is believed that Aryabhatta led the Nalanda University's astronomical observatory in his later years.

The Aryabhatiya

This Sanskrit work, composed in 118 verses, covers topics in arithmetic, algebra, plane and spherical trigonometry, and astronomy. It introduced several advanced concepts far ahead of his time, influencing future Indian scholars like Brahmagupta, Bhaskara I, and many others.

Legacy

  • Regarded as the father of Indian mathematics and astronomy.
  • Inspired generations of scholars in India and the Islamic world during the medieval period.
  • India named its first satellite Aryabhata (launched in 1975) in his honor.
  • His scientific spirit symbolized India's rich intellectual and scientific heritage.
