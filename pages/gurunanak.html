Guru Nanak - Founder of Sikhism

Quick Facts

Time: 1469 - 1539 CE

Origin: Talwandi (present-day Nankana Sahib, Pakistan)

Birth Name: Nanak Dev Ji

Path: Sikhism

Title: Guru Nanak Dev Ji (First Guru of Sikhism)

Core Teachings

1. Three Pillars of Sikhism

  • Naam Japna: Remembering and chanting God's name (Waheguru) regularly.
  • Kirat Karni: Honest living and earning through hard work.
  • Vand Chakna: Sharing with the needy and practicing charity.

2. Key Principles

  • Equality: All humans are equal, regardless of caste, religion, or gender.
  • One God: Belief in one universal God who is shapeless, timeless, and beyond birth or death.
  • Service (Seva): Helping others selflessly is a way to serve God.
  • Reject Rituals: He opposed empty rituals, idol worship, and superstitions.

Spiritual Journey

Guru Nanak traveled across India, Tibet, Mecca, and Arabia, spreading messages of love, unity, and devotion to one God. He composed hymns in the form of shabads, many of which are part of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism.

Legacy

  • Founded the Sikh religion, which now has millions of followers worldwide.
  • Promoted universal brotherhood, humility, and oneness of humanity.
  • Set the foundation for the Ten Sikh Gurus who followed him.
  • His teachings laid the path for a community based on justice, service, and equality.
