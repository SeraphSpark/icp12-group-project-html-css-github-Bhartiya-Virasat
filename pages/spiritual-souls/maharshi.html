Sri Ramana Maharshi - The Sage of Arunachala

Quick Facts

Time: 1879 - 1950 CE

Origin: Tiruchuli, Tamil Nadu, India

Birth Name: Venkataraman Iyer

Path: Advaita Vedanta (Non-dualism)

Title: Bhagavan Sri Ramana Maharshi

Core Teachings

1. Self-Inquiry (Atma Vichara)

  • Realization comes not by effort but by stillness and silence of the mind.

2. True Nature is the Self

  • The Self (Atman) is pure awareness, beyond body and mind.
  • Freedom comes by realizing that we are not the ego but the eternal Self.

3. Silence is Teaching

  • He often taught through silence, stating that truth is beyond words.
  • Presence alone could calm and awaken seekers without formal instruction.

Spiritual Journey

At age 16, Ramana had a spontaneous death-like experience and realized the eternal nature of the Self. He left home and went to Arunachala Hill in Tiruvannamalai, where he lived for the rest of his life. He became a silent sage, drawing seekers from across the world who came in search of peace, enlightenment, and inner freedom.

Legacy

  • His teachings remain a cornerstone of non-dual philosophy and self-realization.
  • Established the Sri Ramanasramam in Tiruvannamalai, which continues to attract spiritual seekers.
  • Emphasized simplicity, inner silence, and Self-awareness as the true path to liberation.
  • Inspired many global teachers and movements in Advaita and meditation practices.
