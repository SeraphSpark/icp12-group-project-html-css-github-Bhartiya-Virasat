Introduction & Divine Identity

Lord Vitthal, also known as Vithoba or Panduranga, is a beloved form of Lord Vishnu and Krishna, deeply venerated in the Indian state of Maharashtra and beyond. He stands as the divine symbol of *bhakti* (devotion), humility, and inner surrender. The deity is especially associated with the Varkari tradition, a centuries-old spiritual movement centered on devotion, community service, and pilgrimage. Lord Vitthal is not just a deity of temples but a presence in the heart of every simple devotee who lives a life of honesty, love, and remembrance of God. Temples, Festivals, and Devotion

The principal temple of Lord Vitthal is in Pandharpur, Maharashtra, located on the banks of the Chandrabhaga River. This temple becomes the epicenter of divine celebration during the *Ashadhi Ekadashi* and *Kartik Ekadashi* festivals, when millions of *Varkaris* (pilgrims) walk hundreds of kilometers in the *Wari* pilgrimage chanting his name and singing *Abhangas*.
