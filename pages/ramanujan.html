Srinivasa Ramanujan - The Mathematical Genius

Quick Facts

Time: 1887 - 1920 CE

Origin: Erode, Tamil Nadu, India

Field: Pure Mathematics

Known For: Infinite series, number theory, continued fractions, partitions

Early Life & Education

Born into a humble Brahmin family, Ramanujan displayed an extraordinary mathematical ability from a young age, despite limited formal training. He mastered advanced trigonometry by age 13 and began developing his own theorems without access to modern resources.

Major Contributions

1. Infinite Series & Number Theory

  • Developed new formulas for œÄ (pi) using infinite series.
  • Worked on highly composite numbers, prime numbers, and partition functions.
  • Introduced Ramanujan's tau function, mock theta functions, and modular equations.

2. Hardy-Ramanujan Collaboration

  • In 1914, British mathematician G.H. Hardy invited him to Cambridge University.
  • Together, they produced several landmark papers in number theory and analysis.

3. Intuitive Mathematics

  • Ramanujan claimed many results came to him in dreams, often crediting the goddess Namagiri for inspiration.
  • His notebooks contain thousands of results, many still being researched today.

Legacy

  • Recognized posthumously as one of the greatest mathematicians of all time.
  • His birthday, December 22 , is celebrated as National Mathematics Day in India.
  • Inspiration for the film "The Man Who Knew Infinity" (2015).
  • Left behind 3 notebooks and a "lost notebook" that continues to impact modern mathematics.

Final Years

Despite suffering from health issues due to climate, diet, and workload, Ramanujan returned to India in 1919 and passed away at the young age of 32 in 1920. His deep and intuitive grasp of mathematics remains unmatched.
