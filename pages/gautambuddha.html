Gautam Buddha - The Enlightened One

Quick Facts

Time: ~5th-6th century BCE

Origin: Lumbini (modern-day Nepal)

Birth Name: Siddhartha Gautama

Path: Buddhism

Title: Buddha ("The Enlightened One")

Core Teachings

1. The Four Noble Truths

  • Dukkha: Life involves suffering ‚Äì birth, sickness, aging, and death are all painful.
  • Samudaya: Suffering is caused by craving, attachment, and desire.
  • Nirodha: Suffering can end by letting go of desires.
  • Magga: The Eightfold Path is the way to end suffering.

2. The Noble Eightfold Path

  • Right View
  • Right Intention
  • Right Speech
  • Right Action
  • Right Livelihood
  • Right Effort
  • Right Mindfulness
  • Right Concentration

3. Core Philosophies

  • Anicca: Everything is impermanent and always changing.
  • Dukkha: Suffering exists in all forms of life.
  • Anatta: There is no permanent "self" or soul.

Detachment from Desires

Buddha taught that craving and attachment lead to suffering. By letting go of desires, ego, and attachments, one can achieve inner peace and reach Nirvana ‚Äî a state of liberation and freedom from rebirth.

Legacy

  • Founded Buddhism, which spread across Asia and later the world.
  • Stressed non-violence, compassion, and mindfulness.
  • His teachings are preserved in texts like the Tripitaka.
  • Inspired millions toward inner awakening and spiritual freedom.
